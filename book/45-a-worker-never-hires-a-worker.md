# 45. A worker never hires a worker

**What this chapter settles:** the local form that keeps every hiring decision with you, the owner.

## The form leaves only local facts blank

Every restaurant fills in its own copy of this form. **The rule is already printed because
it does not change from one kitchen to another.** The blanks only identify the restaurant
and confirm that its owner has accepted the rule.

```
RESTAURANT: ______________________________________

[ ] The owner is the only role with hiring authority here.
[ ] No worker may hire another worker, including temporary cover.
[ ] The area manager has no hiring authority here.
[ ] A hire made by a worker is invalid and must be undone through this
    restaurant's normal correction process.
[ ] This completed form stays in this restaurant.

OWNER'S CONFIRMATION: ____________________________
```

Do not put a person's name into the printed book. The completed form belongs to one
restaurant. **It does not travel to another restaurant**, because the next restaurant must
fill in and keep its own form.

## Hiring authority stops with the owner

The word worker means every role below you, without exception. A more senior worker does not
become an owner. A trusted worker does not become an owner. **No worker may make the decision
that brings another worker into the restaurant.**

A worker may find possible people for you to consider. That does not pass on hiring
authority. Finding possibilities is a task. Choosing whether someone joins the restaurant
is the hiring decision, and that decision remains yours.

## Temporary cover is still a hire

Calling someone temporary does not change what happened. If the decision brings a new
worker into the restaurant, it is hiring. **A short shift, an urgent need or temporary cover
does not create an exception.** The owner still decides.

This is tempting because a worker can see the empty place before you do. Speed does not
move responsibility. A quick hire by a worker is still an invalid hire.

## The chain cannot grow below you

If one worker could hire, the new worker could claim the same power and hire again. Each new
worker could add another. Soon no one could say which owner accepted responsibility for the
people in the restaurant.

**The chain stops at you.** You may not pass hiring authority to a worker, because passing
it once removes the only stopping point.

## The area manager remains outside hiring

The area manager works at chain level. That wider view does not make the area manager the
owner of a restaurant. **The area manager may not hire for a restaurant**, even when helping
several restaurants at once. The same stopping point holds: the owner decides.

## An invalid hire must be undone

A hiring decision made by any worker has no standing. Do not keep the new worker in place
because the mistake has already happened. **Undo the hire through the restaurant's normal
correction process.** The owner may then consider what the restaurant needs and make a
separate hiring decision.

---

## The rules, flat

*The same rules as above, with the explanation removed. This is the part the appliance
obeys.*

1. Fill in one hiring-authority form locally for each restaurant.
2. Record the owner as the only role with hiring authority.
3. Keep the completed form in its restaurant. Never send it to another restaurant.
4. Treat every role below the owner as a worker for this rule, without exception.
5. Never let a worker hire another worker, including temporary cover.
6. Allow a worker to find possibilities only when the hiring decision remains with the owner.
7. Never pass hiring authority to a worker. Stop the chain at the owner.
8. Never give the area manager hiring authority for a restaurant.
9. Treat every hire made by a worker as invalid and undo it through the restaurant's normal correction process.
