# 20. Serving it

**What this chapter settles:** how a finished dish leaves the kitchen, reaches the customer, and receives the two serving stamps without giving the waiter another role.

## Service begins only after both checks pass

The dish is cooked, but that alone does not make it ready to serve. **It may leave the kitchen only after the head chef's process check and the taster's quality check have both passed.** The taster's word is final.

Chapter 19 settles how those checks are made. Here, the waiter needs only their outcome. If either check has not passed, or cannot be made, the dish stays in the kitchen. No checker, no service. Serving an unknown dish would break the promise of the same dish every time.

Before the dish leaves, the hygiene checks from chapter 13 run automatically. **The waiter does not perform those checks by hand and may never bypass them.** If that protection does not allow the dish to leave, the waiter does not carry it out.

## The waiter carries the dish and never judges it

The waiter now completes the second half of the role from chapter 9. The first half was taking the order. This half is carrying the finished, checked dish from the kitchen back to the customer.

Follow these steps in order.

1. **Confirm that both required verdicts have passed.** Do not repeat either check or replace a missing verdict with confidence.
2. **Let the automatic hygiene checks act before the dish leaves the kitchen.** Do not perform them by hand or go around them.
3. **Carry the approved dish to the customer and place it for service.** Carry this dish as it was checked. Do not cook it, alter it or offer a quality verdict.
4. **Add the serving stamps to the operations log.** Record only what actually happened.

The waiter carries and serves. The waiter does not cook, judge quality, take money or collect plates. **Serving ends when the dish is placed and the customer's satisfaction is noted.** Chapter 21 handles the money. Chapter 22 handles the plates.

## Two stamps record the completed service

Add each stamp to the end of the operations log. Never rewrite an earlier entry, because the log is the diary of what actually happened.

4. **It was served — when, and by whom.** Record when it was served and record the waiter by role, never by personal name.
5. **Whether the customer was satisfied.** Record the answer truthfully. A dissatisfied customer does not make the record wrong; hiding that answer does.

These stamps record the hand-off and its result. They do not turn the waiter into a taster.

## Missing permission keeps the dish in the kitchen

Check failures in this order.

1. **One verdict is missing or did not pass.** Do not serve.
2. **The automatic hygiene checks do not allow the dish to leave.** Do not bypass them.
3. **The waiter starts judging or altering the dish.** Return to the waiter's boundary: carry and serve only.
4. **A serving stamp is missing or flattering.** Add what actually happened to the end of the log; never improve the past.

## Never turn carrying into approval

It is tempting for the person holding the plate to say that it looks good enough. Do not. **A waiter's confidence is not a quality verdict.** Only the taster gives that verdict, and the dish does not move without it.

## The rules, flat

*The same rules as above, with the explanation removed. This is the part the appliance obeys.*

1. Serve a dish only after the head chef's process check and the taster's quality check have both passed. Treat the taster's word as final.
2. Keep the dish in the kitchen whenever either required check has not passed or cannot be made. No checker, no service.
3. Let the automatic hygiene checks run before the dish leaves the kitchen. Never perform them by hand or bypass them.
4. Have the waiter carry the finished, checked dish from the kitchen to the customer and place it for service.
5. Keep the waiter to carrying and serving. Never let the waiter cook, alter the dish, give a quality verdict, take money or collect plates.
6. Add stamp 4 to the end of the operations log: **It was served — when, and by whom.** Record when it was served and identify the waiter by role, never by personal name.
7. Add stamp 5 to the end of the operations log: **Whether the customer was satisfied.** Record the answer truthfully.
8. Never rewrite an earlier log entry. Add only what actually happened to the end.
