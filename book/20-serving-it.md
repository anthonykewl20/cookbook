# 20. Serving it

**What this chapter settles:** how the waiter collects an approved dish, carries it to the customer, and records that service is complete.

## The pass counter makes the hand-off visible

The finished dish does not appear in the waiter's hands. Chapter 19 leaves an approved dish on the pass counter, which the restaurant already has from chapter 4. **The waiter collects it from that counter.** This is the hand-off between the kitchen and the dining room.

Only an approved dish reaches this hand-off. The head chef's process check and the taster's quality check must both have passed. The taster's word is final. If either check has not passed or cannot be made, the dish stays in the kitchen. No checker, no service.

## Automatic protection acts before the dish leaves

Before the dish leaves the kitchen, the hygiene checks from chapter 13 run automatically. **The waiter does not perform them by hand and does not bypass them.** If that protection stops the dish, the waiter leaves it in the kitchen.

The waiter does not replace a missing check with confidence. A dish that has not passed every required check is unknown, and serving it would break the promise of the same dish every time.

## The waiter carries and serves, then stops

The waiter now completes the second half of the role settled in chapter 9. The first half was taking the order. **This half is carrying the finished, checked dish back to the customer.**

Follow these steps in order.

1. **Collect the approved dish from the pass counter.** Do not take a dish that is still waiting for either check.
2. **Carry it to the customer and place it for service.** Carry the dish as it was checked. Do not cook it, alter it or give a quality verdict.
3. **Write stamp 4 in the operations log.** Once that stamp is written, service is done and payment may begin.
4. **Write stamp 5 in the operations log.** Record truthfully whether the customer was satisfied.

The waiter does not take the money or collect the plates. Chapter 21 gives the money to the cashier. Chapter 22 gives the plates to the dishwasher. This chapter's work ends after the dish is placed and the customer's satisfaction is noted.

## Two stamps show that service happened

Add both stamps to the end of the operations log. Never rewrite an earlier entry, because the log is the diary of what actually happened.

4. **It was served — when, and by whom.** Record when it was served and record the waiter by role, never by personal name. This stamp is also the cashier's signal. Once it appears, the cashier knows that payment may begin.
5. **Whether the customer was satisfied.** Record the answer truthfully. A dissatisfied customer is still what happened, so it belongs in the log.

These stamps record service. They do not turn the waiter into a taster.

## Never turn carrying into approval

It is tempting for the person holding the dish to decide that it looks good enough. Do not. **The waiter carries the dish; the taster gives the verdict.** Only the taster gives the final quality verdict, and no dish moves without it.

## The rules, flat

*The same rules as above, with the explanation removed. This is the part the appliance obeys.*

1. Serve a dish only after the head chef's process check and the taster's quality check have both passed. Treat the taster's word as final.
2. Keep the dish in the kitchen whenever either required check has not passed or cannot be made. No checker, no service.
3. Run the automatic hygiene checks before the dish leaves the kitchen. Never have the waiter perform them by hand or bypass them.
4. Have the waiter collect the approved dish from the pass counter, carry it to the customer and place it for service.
5. Keep the waiter to carrying and serving. Never let the waiter cook or alter the dish, give a quality verdict, take money or collect plates.
6. Add stamp 4 to the end of the operations log: **It was served — when, and by whom.** Record when it was served and identify the waiter by role, never by personal name.
7. Treat stamp 4 as the signal that service is done and payment may begin.
8. Add stamp 5 to the end of the operations log: **Whether the customer was satisfied.** Record the answer truthfully.
9. Never rewrite an earlier log entry. Add only what actually happened to the end.
