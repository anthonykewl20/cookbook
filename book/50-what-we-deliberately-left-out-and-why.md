# 50. What we deliberately left out, and why

**What this chapter settles:** what this book leaves out on purpose, and why none of it is needed to make the same dish every time.

## What is absent was chosen carefully

A useful manual does not contain everything its owner knows. It contains what every
restaurant needs in order to keep the promise.

**An omission is deliberate only when the dish remains repeatable without it.** Leaving out
words that belong to one trade, one owner or one restaurant keeps the manual clear and
steady. Leaving out an instruction that changes the result would be a fault, not a choice.

## You never need a programmer's vocabulary

This book is written for a non-technical owner. Every word that only a programmer needs was
left out of every page on purpose.

**You should be able to run the chain without first learning another trade's language.** The
appliance may need exact words for a local action. Those words belong beside that action.
They do not belong in the explanation that helps you decide what should happen.

## The pictures are examples rather than law

This book uses a restaurant, a kitchen, a dish, chefs, a taster, an appliance, a recipe, a
shopping list, a telephone game, and a building with doors. **That agreed set is one owner's
worked example, not required language for every owner.**

Adding competing pictures would make the same promise change shape from page to page. The
book therefore uses no others. Chapter 1 supplies the conversation that settles your local
words; this chapter does not replace that conversation.

## Local facts stay in their restaurant

Staff names are absent. So are the commands used in one restaurant and the exact addresses
of its things inside the appliance. **They are filled in locally because they describe one
place, not the chain's lasting way of working.**

The same is true of records and pages completed for one restaurant. The instructions in
this book travel. Records never do. Putting local facts into the travelling manual would
make every change of staff or place look like a change to the promise.

## One door lets the manual improve

Static does not mean frozen. A manual with no door cannot improve. A manual with several
doors changes differently in different hands.

**This manual has exactly one door.** A needed improvement can pass through it and be made
once for everybody. Nothing else may quietly bend the words. The choice was one door, not
zero, so the manual can improve without rotting.

## What remains is what makes the dish repeatable

The people vary. The result must not. **The manual is the only thing allowed to hold still.**

Personal words, staff names, local commands and local records do not make the dish
repeatable. The travelling instructions do. That is the test behind every deliberate
exclusion: what is left out may change from place to place, while what remains must hold the
result steady.

## Never disguise a missing promise as a choice

It is tempting to call every gap deliberate after the fact. Do not. **If something missing
can change the dish, it is a fault and must go through the one door.** This chapter explains
the book's boundaries. It does not turn mistakes elsewhere into design.

---

## The rules, flat

*The same rules as above, with the explanation removed. This is the part the appliance
obeys.*

1. Write every travelling instruction in words a non-technical owner can understand. Keep
   programmers' vocabulary out of every page.
2. Use only the agreed pictures: restaurant, kitchen, dish, chefs, taster, appliance, recipe,
   shopping list, telephone game, and building with doors.
3. Treat those pictures as one owner's worked example, never as required language for every
   owner.
4. Settle each owner's words through the conversation in Chapter 1. Never write those local
   words back into this book.
5. Keep staff names, local commands and exact addresses inside the appliance in their local
   restaurant. Never put them into the travelling manual.
6. Let the instructions in this book travel. Keep records and locally completed pages in
   their restaurant.
7. Keep the manual static but not frozen. Change it through exactly one door.
8. Leave out only what can vary without changing the dish.
9. Treat any missing promise that can change the dish as a fault. Never call it a deliberate
   exclusion.
