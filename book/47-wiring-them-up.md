# 47. Wiring them up

**What this chapter settles:** how to connect every locally named role to the kitchen where it will work, without sending one restaurant's answers to another.

## A name alone does not make a working role

Your staff form gives each role a local name. That name is useful only when the kitchen can
call the role in, the role can find its written job and the kitchen can receive its work
back.

**Wiring joins those three things in one restaurant.** It does not choose the role's name
again. It uses the name already settled on the local staff form.

## Begin with every role the book supplies

Do not begin with an empty list. **Wire up the head chef, cook, taster, doctor, waiter,
cashier and dishwasher. Keep prep cook ready as the supplied growth role.** These are the
starting roles even when your kitchen uses different words for them.

A role may not yet have separate hands to fill it. That does not remove its place from the
form. The form connects roles, not hires people.

## Fill the connections in this kitchen

Take the local name and the way of calling in each role from the staff form. Then make sure
that the role can find the written job it follows and knows how to return its work to the
kitchen.

Fill in one row for every supplied role:

| Role supplied by the book | Local role name from the staff form | The kitchen can call this role in | The role can find its written job | The role can return its work |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Head chef |  | yes / no | yes / no | yes / no |
| Cook |  | yes / no | yes / no | yes / no |
| Taster |  | yes / no | yes / no | yes / no |
| Doctor |  | yes / no | yes / no | yes / no |
| Waiter |  | yes / no | yes / no | yes / no |
| Cashier |  | yes / no | yes / no | yes / no |
| Dishwasher |  | yes / no | yes / no | yes / no |
| Prep cook — growth role |  | yes / no | yes / no | yes / no |

If a row says no, finish that connection in this kitchen. Do not change the written job to
make the row easier. The job belongs to the shared manual; this form only connects the local
role to it.

## Every filled answer stays where it was made

Each restaurant fills in its own copy because each one has its own role words and its own
way of calling staff in. **The blank form may be shared, but a filled form never travels.**
Keep it in the restaurant that answered it. Never copy its answers into the book or into
another restaurant.

The next restaurant starts from the same supplied roles and fills in a fresh copy. One
kitchen's working connections are not instructions for the chain.

## Staff wiring does not fit out the building

This form connects the staff roles. It does not put the benches, doorman, hygiene checks or
till in place. **Chapter 49 fits out the restaurant with the things the book's rules depend
on.** Keeping that work separate prevents a local staff form from becoming a second manual.

## Never put a person where a role belongs

A person's name may make the form feel more complete today. Tomorrow, that person may be
gone and the connection will point at yesterday.

**Name the role, never the person.** A role tells the next stranger which part of the kitchen
is theirs. Use the local role name in every row, even when one person fills several roles.

## The rules, flat

*The same rules as above, with the explanation removed. This is the part the appliance
obeys.*

1. Wire up every role supplied by the book: head chef, cook, taster, doctor, waiter, cashier,
   dishwasher and prep cook.
2. Keep prep cook on the form as the supplied growth role, even before separate hands fill it.
3. Copy each local role name and its way of being called in from that kitchen's staff form.
4. Confirm that each role can be called in, can find its written job and can return its work.
5. Finish every missing local connection without changing the role's written job.
6. Fill in and keep one wiring form in each restaurant. Share only the blank form. Never send
   a filled form or its answers to another restaurant or write them into this book.
7. Use the local role name in every row. Name the role, never the person.
8. Keep staff wiring separate from fitting out the restaurant. Leave the benches, doorman,
   hygiene checks and till to Chapter 49.
