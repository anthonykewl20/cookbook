# 22. Clearing down

**What this chapter settles:** how to collect the plates after service, have the dishwasher wash them, and return everything ready for the next service.

## Clearing down starts after the customer has paid

Clearing down is the last work on one order. **Begin only after the dish has been served and the money has been taken and recorded.** Until then, the waiter and cashier still have their own work to finish.

Do not collect the plates early to make the table look finished. Service comes first, payment follows, and only then does clearing down begin. This order keeps the three jobs separate and shows exactly where the order has reached.

## The plates pass to the dishwasher and come back clean

Follow these steps in order.

1. **Have the plates collected and handed to the dishwasher.** Once that has happened, add stamp 6 to the end of the operations log.
2. **Have the dishwasher clean the plates properly.** They must be clean for the next service, because the dishwasher role exists to make sure that service starts clean.
3. **Put everything that was washed back where it belongs.** Washing is not finished while clean plates are left beside the machine or placed somewhere a stranger would not expect to find them.
4. **Add stamp 7 to the end of the operations log.** Write it only after the washing and putting back have both happened.

The plates are utensils already placed in the restaurant. The dishwashing machine is equipment. **The dishwasher is the role responsible for the work, not the machine that helps with it.**

## The dishwasher stops at the edge of clearing down

The dishwasher clears up after service and puts back what it washed where it belongs. It must not touch the order, the cooking or a checking verdict. Those jobs already belonged to other roles, and washing the plates does not reopen them.

One person may cover the dishwasher while also holding another role, provided every required separation still holds. When doing this work, that person acts as the dishwasher. The operations log names the dishwasher role, never the person or the other role they also hold. Roles survive a change of staff; personal names do not.

## Two final stamps finish the life of the order

Add these entries to the end of the operations log, after each event happens:

6. **The plates were collected and handed to the dishwasher.**
7. **The dishwasher cleaned them properly, and put everything back in its place.**

The log is a diary of what actually happened. Never rewrite an earlier entry to insert either stamp, and never write both in advance. Stamp 6 records the hand-off. Stamp 7 records that the washing and putting back are complete.

## Four checks find an unfinished clear-down

Check these failures in order.

1. **Clearing began before service or payment was complete.** Wait until the dish has been served and the money has been taken and recorded.
2. **A stamp was written before its work happened.** Leave the earlier entry alone and record only what actually happens next.
3. **Clean plates were not put back.** Return everything washed to its proper place before adding stamp 7.
4. **The entry names a person or a different role.** Name the work under the dishwasher role.

## Never let washing reach back into the order

One person wearing several hats can make the boundaries feel unimportant. They are not. **The dishwasher handles clearing down only.** It never changes the order, touches the cooking or supplies a checking verdict.

---

## The rules, flat

*The same rules as above, with the explanation removed. This is the part the appliance obeys.*

1. Begin clearing down only after the dish has been served and the money has been taken and recorded.
2. Collect the plates and hand them to the dishwasher.
3. Add stamp 6 to the end of the operations log after the hand-off: **The plates were collected and handed to the dishwasher.**
4. Have the dishwasher clean the plates properly and put everything it washed back where it belongs.
5. Add stamp 7 to the end of the operations log only after the washing and putting back are complete: **The dishwasher cleaned them properly, and put everything back in its place.**
6. Treat the dishwashing machine as equipment and the dishwasher as the role responsible for clearing down.
7. Keep the dishwasher away from the order, the cooking and every checking verdict.
8. Allow another role to cover the dishwasher only where every required separation still holds. Record the work under the dishwasher role, never by person or by another role that person holds.
9. Add stamps 6 and 7 only to the end of the operations log. Never rewrite an earlier entry or write a stamp before its work happens.
