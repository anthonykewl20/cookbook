# 36. Opening a new restaurant

**What this chapter settles:** what to carry into a new restaurant, and what must begin there.

## The manual arrives unchanged

A new restaurant does not begin by remembering how another one works. **It inherits the
chain's static manual unchanged.** The recipes, house rules, taster's checklist and job
descriptions all arrive in the words already agreed for the chain.

The book itself arrives in the same way it did at the first restaurant. Install it on the
new restaurant's appliance. Never copy its words into local notes. A copy can drift away
from the book, leaving the new kitchen to follow a promise the chain never made.

This is the line from Part Two put to work at the door: **instructions travel. Records never
do.** Instructions tell a kitchen what should happen. Records belong to what has happened
in one place, so carrying them over would give the new restaurant somebody else's past.

## The restaurant itself starts blank

Nothing owned or lived comes through the door. The new restaurant starts with its own
building, furniture, equipment, utensils, stock and money. It hires its own people into the
roles named by the travelling job descriptions. People are not borrowed from another
restaurant, even though the jobs are shared across the chain.

Its stocktake starts as a photograph of this restaurant alone. Its operations log starts as
an empty diary. Never bring in another restaurant's stocktake or log, and never combine the
records of several restaurants. A borrowed photograph lies about what is here. A borrowed
diary lies about what happened here.

## The menu is chosen here

The menu is the one local instruction. **A new restaurant does not inherit another
restaurant's menu.** It chooses its own menu from the chain's dishes. It may choose fewer,
never different ones. The recipes for every dish it does choose still arrive unchanged.

## Arrival changes nothing in the manual

A different building may make a small rewrite look sensible. Do not make it. **A restaurant
that rewrites a travelling instruction on arrival is outside the chain.** It is no longer
making the chain's promise, even if its new words sound better.

If the restaurant cannot serve a chain dish as written, leave that dish off its local menu.
Do not change its recipe, the house rules, the taster's checklist or a job description to
make the place fit.

## Open the restaurant in four steps

1. Install the book on the new restaurant's appliance.
2. Bring in the recipes, house rules, taster's checklist and job descriptions unchanged.
3. Begin the restaurant's own place, people, stocktake and operations log locally, with no
   record carried over.
4. Choose its menu from the chain's dishes: fewer if needed, never different.

---

## The rules, flat

*The same rules as above, with the explanation removed. This is the part the appliance
obeys.*

1. Install the book on each new restaurant's appliance. Never copy it into local notes.
2. Carry the recipes, house rules, taster's checklist and job descriptions into each new
   restaurant unchanged.
3. Start each new restaurant with its own building, furniture, equipment, utensils, stock,
   money and locally hired people.
4. Take a local stocktake of the new restaurant. Never carry in or combine another
   restaurant's stocktake.
5. Start the new restaurant's operations log empty. Never carry in or combine another
   restaurant's log.
6. Let each new restaurant choose its own menu from the chain's dishes. Allow fewer dishes,
   never different ones, and never inherit another restaurant's menu.
7. Keep every travelling instruction unchanged on arrival. Treat a restaurant that rewrites
   one as outside the chain.
