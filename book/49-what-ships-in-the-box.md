# 49. What ships in the box

**What this chapter settles:** which working tools arrive with the book, and which rule each one makes true.

## The box arrives ready for service

Installing the book does more than put words on the wall. **It brings with it the working
restaurant those words depend on.** The fittings from Part Two are already joined up before
Part Three begins to use them.

Each cook has a separate bench. The shared main kitchen, pass counter and till are in place.
The doorman is at the door. The hygiene checks, safe and fire exit work. The stocktake and
operations log are ready as two separate records.

Nothing has to be remembered into existence on the first day of service.

## Every tool holds up a settled rule

Each thing is in the box because a rule would fail without it.

- **The separate benches and the doorman** make “never share a bench for one dish” true. A
  cook works at one separate bench, and the doorman keeps that work out of the shared main
  kitchen. Chapter 48 gives the doorman's full job.
- **The pass counter** makes the hand-off real. A finished dish leaves its maker's bench
  before judgment and has one known place to wait for checking.
- **The till** holds up “the takings go in the till”. It gives money one settled place instead
  of wherever a cashier happens to put it.
- **The hygiene checks** make “nothing leaves the kitchen unchecked” true. They act before
  every dish goes out, apart from the taster's verdict.
- **The safe** keeps what must not be seen out of sight. **The fire exit** keeps a safe way
  out when danger is inside.

These are not decorations beside the rules. They are what let the restaurant obey them.

## The diary has a place where stamps are written

The operations log is not an idea or a loose pile of notes. **It is a ledger: a record the
appliance keeps for the restaurant.** Every order stamp and every other account of what
actually happened is written there, in the order it happened.

The ledger holds up “the log is never rewritten, only added to at the end”. It accepts each
new entry only at the end and leaves every earlier entry as it was. A correction is another
entry at the end, because changing an old entry would change the restaurant's past.

## The photograph never becomes the diary

The stocktake is the restaurant's current photograph. A new photograph replaces the old one
when the answer changes. **The appliance keeps it apart from the operations log**, because a
photograph and a diary are two records and never one thing.

That separation holds up both rules at once. The photograph can be replaced, while the diary
can keep growing without losing its past.

## A remembered rule is not enforced

**A rule that depends on somebody remembering it is not enforced.** Staff change. A sentence
on the wall can be missed. These tools still give the dish one bench, refuse the shared main
kitchen, keep the diary's past and check what leaves.

That is why they ship already working instead of being left as advice. They do their jobs
whether or not anyone has read the book.

## Never replace a tool with words

A reminder may sound cheaper than a bench, safe, check or doorman. It cannot do their work.
Keep every shipped tool working. **Never replace a tool with words.**

---

## The rules, flat

*The same rules as above, with the explanation removed. This is the part the appliance
obeys.*

1. Ship every restaurant with separate benches, the shared main kitchen, pass counter, till,
   doorman, hygiene checks, safe, fire exit, stocktake and operations log fitted and working
   before service begins.
2. Give each cook one separate bench for a dish. Never share a bench for one dish, and never
   allow a cook's work into the shared main kitchen.
3. Move every finished dish from its maker's bench to the pass counter before judgment. Keep
   it there for checking.
4. Keep the takings in the till.
5. Run the hygiene checks before every dish leaves the kitchen. Keep them apart from the
   taster's verdict.
6. Keep what must not be seen protected in the safe. Keep the fire exit working as a safe way
   out.
7. Keep the operations log as the ledger the appliance keeps for the restaurant. Write every
   stamp and other account of what actually happened there, in the order it happened.
8. Add to the operations log only at the end. Never erase, tidy or rewrite an earlier entry;
   add every correction at the end.
9. Replace the old stocktake when its current answer changes. Keep the stocktake apart from
   the operations log; never merge the photograph with the diary.
10. Keep every shipped tool working whether or not anybody reads or remembers the book.
    Never replace a tool with words.
