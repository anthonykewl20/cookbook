# 46. When someone is off sick

**What this chapter settles:** how each kitchen records sickness cover without breaking a separation or quietly turning cover into hiring.

## Every kitchen fills in its own blank form

Part Six gives you blank forms, not answers. **This sickness cover form belongs to one kitchen and is filled in there**, because only that kitchen knows which role is absent and which role might cover it.

The completed form stays there. It does not go to another restaurant, and one restaurant's answer never becomes the chain's answer. Nothing in this third pile travels.

Use this blank form:

> **Role off sick:** ______  
> **Role proposed to cover it:** ______  
> **Maker will not approve the work:** yes / no  
> **Doctor will not treat what it finds:** yes / no  
> **Nobody will judge their own work:** yes / no  
> **Every required checker is still present:** yes / no  
> **Cover may go ahead:** yes / no  
> **Owner decision needed:** ______

Write roles in the blanks, never people's names. **The form records how the kitchen is arranged, not who a person is.**

## Every separation must survive the cover

Another person may cover the absent role only when every answer about separation is yes. The maker still never approves. The doctor still never treats. Nobody judges their own work.

Convenience cannot turn a no into a yes. If taking the role would make one person cross either side of a separation, leave the role empty. Sickness may remove service. It never removes a required check.

## An empty checking role closes the kitchen

If no valid cover exists for a required checker, **the role stays empty and the kitchen closes**. Anything else would allow a dish to be served without the check the chain promised.

Log the stoppage. This form does not replace that record. It only shows the local cover question and its answer; the diary is handled in Chapter 12.

## Temporary cover never becomes local hiring

Temporary cover is an arrangement of roles during an absence. Hiring is a different matter. **A worker cannot choose or bring in another worker as cover**, even when closing feels costly.

If valid cover would require hiring, the form stops. Chapter 45 settles why a worker never hires a worker. This form does not provide a way around it.

## The owner receives a decision, not a job

When hiring may be needed, write the decision plainly in the last blank: whether to hire. Keep the form in the kitchen. Put only that decision to the owner, because hiring belongs at owner level.

Do not turn it into a list of work for the owner to carry out. The owner decides. The kitchen does not quietly make the decision below and pass the owner the errands afterward.

## Never publish a filled-in form as the answer

One completed sheet may look useful to every restaurant. That is the temptation. Do not copy it across the chain. **The blank form is shared; each answer stays in the kitchen that made it.** Otherwise one local absence and one local arrangement become a rule for places where neither is true.

---

## The rules, flat

*The same rules as above, with the explanation removed. This is the part the appliance
obeys.*

1. Fill in the sickness cover form in the affected kitchen, and keep the completed form there. Never send a completed form to another restaurant.
2. Name people by role on the form. Never write a person's name.
3. Confirm every required separation before allowing cover. Do not let the maker approve, the doctor treat or anybody judge their own work.
4. If any required separation would fail, leave the role empty.
5. If no valid cover exists for a required checker, close the kitchen and log the stoppage.
6. Treat temporary cover as an arrangement of roles, never as permission for a worker to hire or bring in another worker.
7. Keep the form in the kitchen. Put any hiring question to the owner as a decision about whether to hire, never as work to perform.
8. Share the blank form only. Keep every filled-in answer in the kitchen that made it.
