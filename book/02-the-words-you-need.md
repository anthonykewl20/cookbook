# 2. The handful of words you need before you can talk at all

**What this chapter settles:** what five of your kitchen's first agreed words mean, so nobody mistakes one kind of responsibility for another.

## Chapter 1 chose the words and this chapter fixes their meanings

Chapter 1 explains how you arrive at your own words. **You may choose any words that feel natural, but the five meanings below stay fixed.** Otherwise two kitchens could sound alike while expecting different things.

Write your choices down in the kitchen where they were agreed. Keep this book blank on those choices, because another owner may choose different words for the same meanings.

## Your owner word names the person with the final say

The first word means you: the person who owns the place. **The owner makes the decisions that belong to the whole place or chain.** Ownership does not mean doing every job or answering every question. It means that when a choice belongs to the owner, nobody else quietly makes it instead.

The owner may own one restaurant or the whole chain. The meaning stays the same: this is the person whose decision is final.

## Your day-to-day word names the person who keeps one place running

The second word means the person who runs a restaurant from day to day. **They act within the decisions already made.** They settle the ordinary questions of the day and make sure the work has someone responsible for it.

This person does not turn their own work into the owner's work. When the owner's attention is truly needed, what goes upward is a decision for the owner to make, never a task for the owner to perform.

## Your work word names the people who make the dishes

The third word means the people who do the work. In this book's picture, they are the cooks and their work produces a dish. **The word names the people responsible for making what was asked for.**

It does not mean the owner merely because the owner asked. It does not mean the person checking merely because checking is also work. Each word answers a different question: who owns, who runs, who makes and who checks.

## Your checking word names the person who judges the work

The fourth word means the person who checks what the workers made. In this book's picture, that person is the taster. **The person named by this word decides whether the dish is acceptable; the person who made it does not give that verdict on their own dish.** A check made by the same hands that made the work is only the worker's opinion repeated.

This word covers the one checking responsibility agreed here. Other jobs can be named later, when the book needs them.

## Your machine word names the one appliance everyone works on

The fifth word means the machine on which the kitchen's work is done. **It names the one appliance everyone works on, and only that appliance.**

The appliance is none of the people named above. It is the machine they work on. Giving it one clear name lets everyone know which machine a conversation is about.

## Never send the owner a task

It is tempting for the person running the place to pass a difficult job upward and call that ownership. Do not. **The owner's part is the decision that only the owner can make, not the unfinished work of running the day.** If every restaurant sends its unfinished tasks to the owner, the owner ends up running every restaurant day to day and cannot own the chain.

## The rules, flat

*The same rules as above, with the explanation removed. This is the part the appliance
obeys.*

1. Use the locally agreed word for the person who owns the place and has the final say.
2. Use the locally agreed word for the person who keeps one place running day to day.
3. Send the owner decisions that only the owner can make. Never send the owner a task to perform.
4. Use the locally agreed word for the people who make the dishes.
5. Use the locally agreed word for the person who checks whether the work is acceptable.
6. Never let the person who made a dish give the checking verdict on that dish.
7. Use the locally agreed word for the one appliance on which everyone works.
8. Keep the five meanings separate. Never use one agreed word for another meaning.
9. Write the agreed words down only in the kitchen where they were chosen. Never write them into this book.
