# 21. Taking the money

**What this chapter settles:** how the cashier takes payment after service, puts the takings in the till, and records what happened without stepping into anyone else's work.

## Payment follows service and comes before clearing down

Serving is complete and the customer has the dish. The next job is to take the money. **The cashier takes payment only after the dish has been served and before the plates are collected.** This keeps serving, payment and clearing down as three separate pieces of work.

Follow these steps in order.

1. **Wait until the dish has been served.** Do not take over any part of serving it.
2. **Take the money from the customer.** Act as the cashier, even if the same person also works as the waiter or dishwasher.
3. **Put the takings in the till.** Handle both as money.
4. **Add what happened to the end of the operations log.** Record the cashier by role, never by personal name.

Once payment has been taken and recorded, clearing down can begin. Chapter 22 handles the plates and washing. The cashier does not.

## The till remains money while it receives the takings

Chapter 8 already settled the till's place, and its money test takes priority. **Treat the till as money, not equipment, while handling the payment.** Do not count it a second time merely because it is powered.

This chapter does not decide what counts as money or what the restaurant may spend. It handles money that is already present in the furnished restaurant.

## The cashier takes money and touches nothing else

The cashier has one job in this part of service: taking the money. **The cashier does not touch the order, the dish or a checking verdict.** Those belong to other roles.

One person may also work as the waiter or dishwasher where the required separations still hold. The work must still be named by the role doing it. When that person takes payment, the operations log says the cashier did it. A personal name would make the record depend on someone who may not return for the next shift.

## Payment is logged without becoming another stamp

Taking the money is something that actually happened, so it belongs in the operations log. **Add it to the end of the diary as a separate entry.** Never rewrite an earlier entry to make room for it.

An order already has exactly seven stamps. Taking payment is not one of them. Making it an eighth stamp would change the settled life of an order and confuse payment with a hand-off that the seven stamps already describe.

## Check the boundaries when payment goes wrong

Check these failures in order.

1. **The money was taken before service finished.** Wait until the dish has been served.
2. **The till was treated as equipment or counted twice.** Keep it with money.
3. **The cashier touched the order, dish or a verdict.** Return the cashier to taking money only.
4. **The payment was omitted or made into another stamp.** Add a separate entry to the end of the operations log.
5. **The entry names a person.** Name the cashier by role instead.

## Never invent an eighth stamp

It is tempting to make every event in service another order stamp. Do not. **Record the payment, but keep it outside the seven stamps.** The diary can hold what happened without changing the order's settled shape.

---

## The rules, flat

*The same rules as above, with the explanation removed. This is the part the appliance obeys.*

1. Have the cashier take the money from the customer after the dish has been served and before clearing down begins.
2. Put the takings in the till. Treat both as money. Never treat the till as equipment or count it twice.
3. Keep the cashier away from the order, the dish and every checking verdict.
4. Allow the same person to work as cashier, waiter or dishwasher only where every required separation still holds.
5. Name the cashier by role, never by personal name, even when one person holds more than one role.
6. Add the taking of money to the end of the operations log as what actually happened. Never rewrite an earlier entry.
7. Record the payment as a separate entry, never as an eighth order stamp. Keep the order's seven stamps unchanged.
8. Begin clearing down only after the payment has been taken and recorded.
