# Settling a contested claim by proof — R&D for a new chapter beside ch. 25

> **About this file.** Research for a printer's brief. The book is bound and its table of
> contents is locked (`CONTENTS.md:3-5`), so adding a chapter is an owner decision and this
> file does not decide it — it gathers the evidence the owner and the printer need.
>
> **Scope.** A validated house rule, developed in a sister kitchen ("ocask"), is proposed as a
> new chapter placed beside chapter 25 ("Saying something is wrong"). The rule:
> *"Settle contested claims by proof, not by who's speaking. Authority may PROPOSE a claim but
> may not CLOSE a proof-gated one. A proof-gated claim closes only when BOTH hold: (1) an
> acceptance test, sanity-checked by someone other than its author, was named BEFORE the answer
> was produced, and the answer PASSED it; (2) that pass was confirmed by someone independent of
> both the author AND the owner. A routine claim closes on a CITED source and reopens as
> proof-gated the moment it is contested. Expertise is legitimate evidence, not proof."*
>
> **Headline finding.** The rule REINFORCES the book's load-bearing rules — it is almost a
> restatement of invariant 5 ("nobody checks their own work") and invariant 11 ("two verdicts
> from two different heads") applied to *claims* rather than *dishes*. It contradicts nothing
> bound, **provided the printer holds one line**: the rule settles a claim *about reality*; it
> does not touch the owner's sole authority to amend the manual (invariant 4). The book already
> runs on this rule — its own pages are swept rule-by-rule because a holistic verdict was
> *measured* to miss a reversed sentence (`THE-CONTRADICTION-PASS.md:32-34`). Externally, five
> cited studies all check out in direction; one (Kaplan & Irvin) must be labelled
> observational/confounded, not causal.
>
> **Note on the sister-kitchen asset.** A `research/proof-over-authority.md` sourced-literature
> file was reported to live on another branch. **It is present in this worktree** and is a
> high-quality prior pass (it even carries a visible self-correction of the Asch/Milgram
> confusion at lines 71-79). This file builds on it rather than duplicating it; where the two
> disagree, this one says so.

Chapter 25 itself settles: "how anybody in a restaurant raises a fault without quietly changing

kitchen has the right to raise the fault, not the right to rewrite the instruction"** (`:40`).
It closes with: **"The later decision belongs to the owner, not to the kitchen that found the

**The ONE tension — the printer must hold this line or the chapter reverses a settled rule.**

The rule says authority "may not CLOSE a proof-gated claim." Two bound sentences look, at first
glance, like they say the opposite:

- **Invariant 4 (the one door):** "The manual changes through exactly one door: a fault is
  raised from below, written into the log, reported upward, **the owner decides**, and it is
  amended once for everybody." (`press/core-invariants.json:5`)
- **Ch. 25:** "The later decision belongs to the owner, not to the kitchen that found the
  fault." (`book/25-saying-something-is-wrong.md:21`)
- **CONTENTS:** "It changes when the owner changes it, and at no other time and by no other
  hand." (`CONTENTS.md:25-27`)

These are **not contradicted**, because they are about a different question. The owner's
undisputed authority is over **the manual** — what the chain's instructions say. The proposed
rule is over **a claim about reality** — whether something is true. The owner can decide to
rewrite a recipe; the owner cannot, by deciding, make a false claim true. The line that holds
both is the one the printer should hand to the chapter:

> **The owner owns the manual. The owner does not own the facts.**

Phrased that way, the rule *protects* the one door rather than rivalling it: the owner's
amendment still goes through exactly one door, and a proof-gated claim still travels that same
door upward — it just cannot be *closed* at the top by rank. This is the same shape as
`THE-CONTRADICTION-PASS.md:78-83`, where the print manager is "the person most tempted to wave
away an inconvenient flag," so a flag is settled by "a worker that produced neither verdict."

### 1d. The book already runs on this rule (the strongest internal evidence)

Three bound mechanisms *are* "settle by proof, not by authority," already in force:

1. **The contradiction pass.** A page is swept rule-by-rule because a holistic verdict was
   *measured* to certify a page that reversed "the log is never rewritten" — twice
   (`THE-CONTRADICTION-PASS.md:17-25`). The rule it establishes: **"A check that returns one
   verdict for a whole page cannot find a fault that lives in one sentence. Decompose the
   question until each answer has one place to hide."** (`:32-34`). That is the rule's
   "name the test before the answer" in the book's own printing shop.
2. **The seeded entrance exam.** A checker is admitted only by sitting a known-answer test
   (`THE-CONTRADICTION-PASS.md:44-55`) — acceptance by proof, not by reputation.
3. **The measure-every-Enhancement track.** `TICKETS.md:10`: "an Enhancement is measured, or it
   is an opinion." `KNOWN-HOLES.md:78` sends a proposed growth rule to be "probed and measured
   against real work … never asserted into the book on impression." That *is* the proposed rule,
   already adopted as the book's change method.

So the chapter is not importing a foreign idea. It is writing down, as a base-framework
standard, what the book already does to itself.

## 2. EXTERNAL — each cited claim verified against a primary source

Each entry labels the study design honestly. **Observational** = no random assignment, possibly
confounded. **Experimental** = the researcher manipulated the variable.

### 2a. Kaplan & Irvin 2015 — pre-registration and the fall in positive trial outcomes
**CONFIRMED in direction and magnitude. OBSERVATIONAL / CONFOUNDED — not causal.**

- Primary source: Kaplan, R.M. & Irvin, V.L. (2015), *Likelihood of Null Effects of Large NHLBI
  Clinical Trials Has Increased over Time*, PLOS ONE.
  https://journals.plos.org/plosone/article?id=10.1371/journal.pone.0132382 (PMC full text:
  https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC4526697/).
- **Reported numbers check out:** positive results on the primary outcome fell from **17 of 30
  trials (57%) before 2000 to 2 of 25 (8%) after 2000**; prospective registration on
  ClinicalTrials.gov went from ~0% to ~100% across the same break. (Secondary corroboration:
  https://www.bmj.com/content/351/bmj.h4304; https://www.nature.com/news/registered-clinical-trials-make-positive-findings-vanish-1.18181.)
- **Honest label — observational, explicitly confounded.** The authors themselves write that
  "many variables may have changed around the year 2000" (quoted in the paper's limitations).
  The NHLBI also changed its review criteria in that window. The drop is *consistent with*
  pre-registration removing selective reporting; it is not a randomised demonstration of it.
  A follow-up paper — Dickerman, M.A. et al. (2019), *Avoidable flaws in observational
  analyses: an application to the decline in NHLBI trial outcomes*
  (https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC7076561/) — specifically critiques the causal
  reading. **Cite it as observational and say so in the brief.**
- **Use for the rule:** it is still the best-available evidence that *committing the test before
  seeing the result* removes most of the flattering-but-false "wins." The mechanism is
  plausible and widely accepted; the causality is not proven.

### 2b. Milgram — obedience to authority
**CONFIRMED. EXPERIMENTAL (classic lab), with replication caveats.**

- Primary: Milgram, S. (1963), *Behavioral Study of Obedience*, Journal of Abnormal and Social
  Psychology. https://psycnet.apa.org/record/1964-03472-001.
- **~65% of participants** administered the maximum 450-volt shock on the experimenter's
  instruction, against their own judgment (Yale, 1963). Corroborated across multiple
  summaries (https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Milgram_experiment;
  https://www.simplypsychology.org/milgram.html).
- **Honest label — experimental, but era- and context-dependent.** Modern partial replication
  (Burger, 2009) found lower but still substantial obedience; ethics limit full replication.
  The *direction* (authority extracts compliance against private judgment) is robust; the exact
  65% is a 1963 Yale figure.
- **Use for the rule:** this is the authority datum — direct support for "authority may not
  close a proof-gated claim," because authority reliably produces compliance, not truth.

### 2c. Asch — conformity to peers, and the power of one dissenter
**CONFIRMED, and the task's distinction is correct: this is PEER-conformity, NOT authority.**

- Primary: Asch, S.E. (1951/1956), studies of group pressure. Corroboration:
  https://www.simplypsychology.org/asch-conformity.html;
  https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Asch_conformity_experiments; modern replication Franzen et al.
  2023, https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10686423/.
- **~37% average conformity** to an obviously-wrong peer majority on critical trials; ~75%
  conformed at least once. **Adding one dissenting ally cut conformity by ~80% (to roughly
  5%).** Confederates were presented as fellow participants, **not** as authority figures.
- **Honest label — experimental; mechanism distinct from Milgram.** Asch measures going along
  with a *peer majority*; Milgram measures obeying an *authority*. The existing sister-kitchen
  asset already carries this correction visibly (`research/proof-over-authority.md:71-79`).
- **Use for the rule:** Asch belongs to the rule's **independent-confirmation** clause (mechanism
  2: route closure through a dissenter) — one independent checker collapses most of the error.
  It is **mis-used** if cited as "don't defer to authority" (that is Milgram). The printer must
  not conflate the two.

### 2d. Fagan inspection — an independent check catches what the author missed
**CONFIRMED, with a wide range. EXPERIMENTAL / industry-empirical.**

- Primary: Fagan, M.E. (1976), *Design and Code Inspections to Reduce Errors in Program
  Development*. The Fagan-specific detection figures run high: empirical data from thousands of
  inspections cite **70-85% of defects found before testing, rising to >90%** in the best cases
  (https://grokipedia.com/page/Fagan_inspection; IEEE reflection
  https://www.computer.org/csdl/journal/ts/2025/03/10859180/23X96TmJ4Va). Broader syntheses of
  *general* software inspection put the average lower — **~57-60%, ranging from ~20% to ~93%**
  depending on method and reviewers (the sister-kitchen asset's reading,
  `research/proof-over-authority.md:82-92`, citing https://eprints.bournemouth.ac.uk/18183/3/JearyPhalpMilsomHughesWebsterHolroyd.pdf
  and https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0164121224001055).
- **The task's "60-90%"** is a fair span across Fagan-specific and general-inspection figures.
- **Honest label — the high end is Fagan's own structured method under good conditions; the low
  end is inspection in general.** The range itself is a finding: an independent check *works*,
  but its yield is variable, so it is a mitigation, not a guarantee.
- **Use for the rule:** measured backing for "sanity-checked by someone other than its author"
  — the cook never approves the plate, generalised.

### 2e. Brynjolfsson, Hitt & Kim 2011 — data-driven firms outperform
**CONFIRMED. OBSERVATIONAL with an instrumental-variables correction, not an RCT.**

- Primary: Brynjolfsson, E., Hitt, L.M. & Kim, H.H. (2011), *Strength in Numbers: How Does
  Data-Driven Decisionmaking Affect Firm Performance?* SSRN / ICIS 2011.
  https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=1819486 (MIT IDE full PDF:
  https://ide.mit.edu/sites/default/files/publications/2011.12_Brynjolfsson_Hitt_Kim_Strength%20in%20Numbers_302.pdf).
- **Firms adopting data-driven decision-making (DDD) had output/productivity ~5-6% higher** than
  comparable firms, with parallel effects on asset utilisation and market value. The authors use
  instrumental-variables methods to argue it is not reverse causality.
- **Honest label — observational firm-level data, IV-corrected.** Treat the 5-6% as a
  well-identified association, not a clean causal law. (Note: the slogan "HiPPO —
  highest-paid-person's-opinion" often paired with this result is an anecdotal coinage, not a
  measured construct; "DDD firms outperform" is the measured half.)
- **Use for the rule:** measured support for "prefer a run or a measurement to an argument" —
  where a question can be settled by executing something, executing it beats opinion.

### 2f. No cited study failed to check out

All five families survive in direction. The qualifications are about *design strength*
(Kaplan observational; Asch-vs-Milgram mechanism; Fagan's range; DDD observational), not about
the direction flipping. The one to handle most carefully in the chapter is **Kaplan & Irvin**:
use it, but call it observational, because the Dickerman et al. 2019 critique exists and a
discerning taster will know.

## 3. Prior art — how a standards handbook installs a supreme methodological rule

The closest analog is the **ICMJE 2005 registration policy**. In September 2004 the
International Committee of Medical Journal Editors announced that, from July 2005, they would
consider a clinical trial for publication **only if it had been registered in a public registry
before the first patient was enrolled** (https://www.icmje.org/news-and-editorials/update_2005.html;
original editorial https://www.icmje.org/news-and-editorials/clin_trial_sep2004.pdf;
11-year follow-up Zarin et al. 2017, https://www.nejm.org/doi/full/10.1056/NEJMsr1601330).

The shape is exactly the proposed rule's shape: a standards body makes a methodological
discipline a **gate**, not a recommendation. "No registration, no publication" is the
editorial-world version of "no pre-committed test, no closure." The lesson for the cookbook: a
supreme methodological rule is installed by making the thing it gates *unable to proceed*
without it — which is already how the book enforces "no checker, no service" (invariant 6) and
"the taster never wrote the page" (`TASTING-CHECKLIST.md:113-117`). The chapter should frame
"settle by proof" the same way: not as advice, but as a gate a proof-gated claim cannot pass
without the two independent holds.

A second, nearer analog is professional engineering codes (independent verification/review
requirements) and the Cochrane/pre-registration norms more broadly. These are normative
*codes* — ASSERTED as standards, not measured as outcomes. The measured backing is the studies
in §2; the *normative precedent* for writing the rule into a handbook at the top of the
standards stack is the ICMJE move.

## 4. SYNTHESIS — the chapter's shape, example, risk, and the printer's brief

### 4a. Shape in the kitchen voice (~700 body words)

**Working title:** "Settling a contested claim" (or "Settling it by proof, not by rank").
**"What this chapter settles:"** how a claim two people disagree on is closed — by proof, never
by who said it.

Rough sections (each a plain-language statement heading, per `CHAPTER-TEMPLATE.md:18`):

1. **Authority proposes; it does not close.** The owner, the head chef, and any confident
   source may put a claim forward. What they may not do is close it by rank. The owner owns the
   manual (point back to invariant 4 and ch. 25:21); the owner does not own the facts.
2. **Two kinds of claim, two ways to close.** A *routine, cheap-to-reverse* claim closes on a
   cited source — never on bare assertion — and reopens as proof-gated the moment anyone
   contests it. A *contested, high-stakes, or hard-to-reverse* claim is proof-gated: it closes
   only when both independent holds are met.
3. **Name the test before you cook.** The ticket states how the answer will be checked *before*
   a cook produces it. You do not get to invent the pass mark after seeing the dish. (This is
   ch. 19's pre-stated checklist, lifted from dishes to claims.)
4. **Neither the cook nor the owner approves the plate.** The pass is confirmed by someone
   independent of the author *and* of the owner. (Invariant 5 and 11, restated for claims.)
5. **Expertise is evidence, not proof.** The doctor's diagnosis, a senior cook's hunch, an
   outside expert's view — all are legitimate evidence that raises a claim's likelihood. None
   of them close it. (This is the nuance the evidence forces; see 4c.)
6. **The rules, flat.** Imperative, each derivable from the body above
   (`CHAPTER-TEMPLATE.md:26-31`).

**Voice constraints that bear hardest here** (`CHAPTER-TEMPLATE.md:33-50`,
`TASTING-CHECKLIST.md`): no programmer-only words; analogies only from the agreed restaurant
set; short declarative sentences; bold once or twice per section; every doubtful claim carries
its reason in the same breath. **Crucially, the external studies MUST NOT appear in the body** —
the body stays in the restaurant world. The measured backing lives here in `research/`, as the
book already does for its other rules.

### 4b. The worked example — the rule validating itself

The strongest example is the book's own: **this rule was itself validated by proof, not by
authority.** In the sister kitchen, the rule was treated as a hypothesis. Prior art was
gathered, and each finding was marked MEASURED or ASSERTED. The evidence then *corrected the
rule's strong form* in two visible places:

- The medical literature forced "expertise is irrelevant" to become **"expertise is evidence,
  not proof"** (the EBM qualifier, §2a/§2e and `research/proof-over-authority.md:94-114`).
- The Asch datum was re-attributed — from "anti-authority" to **"the value of a designated
  dissenter"** — because Asch is peer-conformity, not obedience (`research/proof-over-authority.md:71-79`).

So the rule is in this book because it *survived being measured and corrected*, not because the
owner said so. That is the self-referential proof, and it is honest: a rule that says "settle
by proof" and arrived by proof is doing what it preaches. (The printer should keep this to two
or three sentences in the body — it is about the reader's restaurant, not our print shop, per
`TASTING-CHECKLIST.md:82-94`.)

### 4c. The main risk, and how to avoid it

**The risk is reversing invariant 4 ("the owner decides") — by making the rule sound like it
dethrones the owner.** A page that "quietly reverses a settled rule while sounding entirely
sensible is the most dangerous page this book can produce, because nothing about it looks wrong"
(`TASTING-CHECKLIST.md:72-75`). This chapter sits closer to that trap than most, because
"authority may not close a claim" and "the owner decides" will *sound* contradictory to a
tired reader.

**Avoidance, in three moves:**

1. State the distinction up front and name it: the owner owns the **manual**; the owner does not
   own the **facts**. Quote invariant 4 and ch. 25:21 verbatim in the brief's "must not
   contradict" list (see 4d).
2. Keep the one door intact — a proof-gated claim still travels the same door upward
   (raised, logged, reported). The rule only says it cannot be *closed* at the top by rank; it
   does not open a second door.
3. Do not overshoot the evidence. The EBM finding (§2a/2e) says proof works *with* expert
   judgment, not *instead of* it. Writing "expertise is worthless" would contradict invariant 7
   (the doctor's expert diagnosis is load-bearing evidence) and go past what the studies show.
   The defensible line is the one the rule already holds: **expertise proposes; it does not close.**

### 4d. The printer's brief (headline)

**The chapter must:**

- Settle "how a contested claim is closed" as a Part Three standard — routine claims close on a
  cited source and reopen when contested; proof-gated claims close only on (1) a pre-named,
  author-independent acceptance test that PASSED, and (2) independent confirmation.
- Treat "settle by proof" as a **gate**, not advice — the same shape as "no checker, no
  service."
- Carry the worked example that the rule arrived by proof (two sentences, in the reader's
  world).
- Keep all measured backing OUT of the body; it lives in `research/`.

**The chapter must NOT contradict (quote verbatim in the brief):**

- `press/core-invariants.json:5` (inv. 4): *"The manual changes through exactly one door: a

  the kitchen that found the fault."*
- `press/core-invariants.json:6` (inv. 5): *"Nobody ever checks their own work…"* — the chapter
  must *restate* this, not weaken it.
- `press/core-invariants.json:8` (inv. 7): *"The doctor diagnoses and prescribes but NEVER

- `book/19-checking-it.md:37`: *"The taster may never reason, 'The process was followed, so the

**Known holes / records that touch it:**

- `KNOWN-HOLES.md:78` — the measure-every-Enhancement track: the rule is the general statement
  of the method the book already uses for its own changes. The chapter should *name* this link.

- `THE-CONTRADICTION-PASS.md:32-34` — the book's own "decompose the question / name the test"
  mechanism, measured on its own pages. Strongest internal precedent.
- `research/proof-over-authority.md:71-79` — the Asch-vs-Milgram correction the chapter must
