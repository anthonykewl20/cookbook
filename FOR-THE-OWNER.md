# For the owner

Decisions only. Each one is a question with a recommendation. Nothing here asks you to read,
check or confirm anything — that is the kitchen's job.

---

## 1. When DeepSeek's credit runs out, do we pay to keep the reader?

**Recommendation: yes, pay for it.**

It was kept on the roster on the argument that its credit was already spent, so using it cost
nothing extra. That argument expires with the credit. Tonight it stopped being the argument
anyway.

On batch four, three checks cleared all five pages — the mechanical list, the taster on seven
questions, and the rule-by-rule sweep, which was proved that same hour against a page with a
planted fault in it. The reader then found a hole all three had missed: the pass counter is
furnished in chapter 4, the running order names it as the place a finished dish is checked,
and no chapter used it at all. Two pages went back and came back better.

**Every check that gives a verdict asks whether what is written is correct. Only the reader
asks whether anything is missing.** That is a different question, and nothing else on the
roster is asking it.

## 2. The cook writes down how the dish tasted. Does that stand?

**Recommendation: yes, it stands as written — the note is evidence, never approval.**

This has been sitting open as needing your call, because the seven stamps came from your own
design and the kitchen should not quietly edit your work. It is now load-bearing: chapters
18, 19 and 20 were all written on the basis chapter 12 already settled — the cook records how
the dish tasted, and that record is evidence of what happened, never a verdict on whether it
may leave the kitchen.

The reason it holds: the objection was that a cook noting the taste looks like the maker
checking their own work. It is not, because the note decides nothing. **A cook who writes
"this came out bitter" has told the truth about what happened. A cook who writes "this is
fine to serve" has given a verdict, and that is banned.** The stamp records the first and
never the second.

Saying no means taking the taste note out of the third stamp and reprinting four chapters.
Saying nothing leaves four bound chapters resting on a decision that was never made.
